Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about the Prime Minister Laptop Scheme

Who is eligible for the Prime Minister Laptop Scheme?

Students enrolled in recognized public sector universities/degree awarding institutions who meet the following criteria:

  • Currently enrolled in undergraduate or graduate programs
  • Maintaining a minimum CGPA of 3.0 or 75% marks
  • Not in receipt of any other laptop scheme scholarship
  • Meeting financial need criteria (for need-based allocation)

Are students from private universities eligible?

Currently, the scheme primarily focuses on students from public sector universities. However, students from selected private universities that are recognized by the Higher Education Commission (HEC) may be eligible based on specific criteria and available quota.

How can I apply for the laptop scheme?

Eligible students can apply through the following process:

  1. Visit the official PM Laptop Scheme portal
  2. Register with your valid student information
  3. Fill out the online application form completely
  4. Upload required documents (CNIC/B-form, university registration card, academic transcripts)
  5. Submit the application before the deadline

What documents are required for application?

The following documents are typically required:

  • CNIC or B-Form copy
  • Recent photograph
  • University registration card
  • Academic transcripts showing CGPA
  • Domicile certificate
  • Income certificate (for need-based applicants)

How are the laptops distributed?

Laptops are distributed through the following process:

  • Applications are verified by university authorities
  • Eligible candidates are shortlisted based on merit and need
  • Distribution ceremonies are organized at universities
  • Selected students receive notification via SMS/email
  • Students must bring original documents for verification at distribution points

What is the typical waiting period after application?

The selection and distribution process typically takes 2-4 months after the application deadline. This includes verification, shortlisting, procurement, and distribution phases. Students will receive updates on the application portal and via official communication channels.

What are the specifications of the laptops provided?

The laptops provided under the scheme typically have the following specifications:

  • Intel Core i5 or equivalent processor
  • 8GB RAM
  • 256GB SSD storage
  • 14-inch HD display
  • Pre-installed licensed operating system
  • Standard warranty of 1-3 years

Note: Specifications may vary slightly based on procurement batches.

What if I face technical issues with my laptop?

If you encounter technical issues with your laptop:

  • Check the warranty documentation for service center details
  • Contact the manufacturer's authorized service centers
  • Reach out to your university's IT department for initial support
  • Contact the scheme helpline for guidance on warranty claims

Always keep your warranty card and purchase documentation safe.

Can I apply if I'm in my final semester?

Yes, final semester students are eligible to apply provided they meet all other eligibility criteria. However, priority may be given to students with more remaining academic time to benefit from the laptop.

Is there an age limit for applicants?

There is no specific age limit for the scheme. Eligibility is primarily based on enrollment in a recognized educational program and meeting the academic performance criteria.
